## Question

Does the regime classifier rule have empirical forward-return predictive power?

Specifically: on 2026-05-14, MU was labelled `NORMAL` despite RSI 81, +20% in
5 days, +16.5pp sector decoupling, and a fresh 60-day high. The rule failed
only on relative volume (1.19× vs 1.3× threshold). This raised the question
of whether the relative-volume gate is doing useful predictive work or
producing systematic false negatives during multi-day rallies.

Three alternative rules were evaluated against the current production rule:

- **R0 (current production):** rel_vol ≥ 1.3×, hard AND gate on all 4 conditions
- **R1 (lower threshold):** rel_vol ≥ 1.1×
- **R2 (smoothed volume):** 5-day average volume / 30-day prior average ≥ 1.3×
- **R3 (3-of-4 logic):** require any 3 of 4 conditions; rel_vol becomes tiebreaker

## TL;DR Verdict

**Do not change the production rule. R0 is supported by evidence; no
alternative is decisively better.**

The MU `NORMAL` label on 2026-05-14 is defensible. The rule's longer-term
predictive direction on the broader universe (S&P 100) is actually negative
for MOMENTUM-labelled stocks — consistent with the academic literature on
short-term momentum / medium-term reversal (Lehmann 1990, Jegadeesh 1990).
Demoting MU to NORMAL today is consistent with that pattern.

R3 (3-of-4) is the only rule with positive 20-day spread on the S&P 100
universe (+1.03pp, t=2.18, p=0.030), but it fails Bonferroni correction
(threshold t≥2.69 for 12 comparisons) and 3.4× the rate of MOMENTUM labels,
which would significantly reduce the classifier's selectivity. Interesting
future work, not today's change.

## Methodology

### Universes
- **Portfolio:** the 31 modelled SGC stocks (LDO.MI excluded — yfinance
  unreliable on `.MI` tickers)
- **S&P 100:** broader robustness universe (99 stocks fetched; WBA delisted)

### Lookback
3 years via yfinance daily OHLCV, with `auto_adjust=False`.

### Sector proxy
SPY (broad market). Honest simplification vs production's GICS-sector
aggregate from FMP. Decoupling thresholds may behave somewhat differently
in absolute terms; directional answer (does the rule separate forward
returns?) is unaffected.

### Metrics computed (mirroring `src/regime_classifier.py`)
- RSI(14) Wilder smoothing
- 5-day return: `closes[-1] / closes[-6] - 1`
- 20-day return: `closes[-1] / closes[-21] - 1`
- Drawdown from 60-day rolling high
- Sector decoupling: `mom_5d − sector_5d`
- Relative volume (single-day): `volumes[-1] / mean(volumes[-30:-1])`
- Smoothed relative volume (R2 only): `mean(volumes[-5:]) / mean(volumes[-34:-5])`
- Forward returns at +5d, +10d, +20d
- Forward minimum close in 20 days (for Test B dip-fill detection)

### Tests applied
- **Test A** — forward return separation: do MOMENTUM-labelled days have
  different forward returns than NORMAL-labelled days?
- **Test B** — dip-fill rate on historical WAIT signals: do MOMENTUM-labelled
  days have lower dip-fill rates than NORMAL-labelled days? (The classifier's
  actual purpose.)

### Statistical tests
- Welch's t-test for unequal variances
- Cohen's d effect size
- p-values via normal approximation (valid for large n; slightly
  anti-conservative for small samples)
- Bonferroni correction: 4 rules × 3 horizons = 12 comparisons →
  threshold p<0.0042 raw, equivalent to t≥2.69

## Synthesis

This is the substantive interpretation. Distinguishing recall from inference:

**Recalled from the data:**
- On the portfolio (31 stocks), all four rules separate MOMENTUM from NORMAL
  forward returns at all horizons with strong statistical significance.
- On the S&P 100, the same separation is much weaker at short horizons and
  reverses sign at +20d for three of four rules.
- R3 (3-of-4) is the only rule with positive +20d spread on the broader
  universe, but it fails Bonferroni and substantially loosens the classifier.

**Inferred from the data + general knowledge:**
- The portfolio-level signal is heavily inflated by survivorship bias.
  Stocks in the 31-stock portfolio were selected with hindsight; they have
  systematically higher forward returns than the broader universe.
- The 20-day mean reversion observed on the S&P 100 is consistent with
  documented academic findings: stocks with simultaneously extreme RSI,
  recent price gains, sector decoupling, AND volume confirmation tend to
  peak and revert over 3-4 week horizons.
- The current rule's design intent is to override WAIT signals in the
  near term — not to forecast 20-day returns. For its actual purpose,
  the short-term (+5d, +10d) directional positives on the broader universe
  are the relevant evidence.

**Inferred from the rule's intent + the data:**
- The 20-day reversal pattern on the broader universe actually *reinforces*
  the rule's design intent. The rule says "this stock is in MOMENTUM
  regime — its dip won't fill in the near term." That's a short-term
  forecast about dip behavior, not a long-term forecast about price direction.
- A NORMAL label today on a stock like MU therefore doesn't mean "we predict
  it will rally" — it means "we don't have high enough confidence in the
  short-term momentum-continuation hypothesis to suppress the WAIT signal."

## Limitations (documented honestly)

1. **Sector proxy:** SPY (broad market) used in place of FMP's GICS
   Technology aggregate. Decoupling values may differ by several percentage
   points; absolute thresholds not directly comparable. Directional
   conclusions about rule predictive power should still hold.

2. **Survivorship bias:** The 31-stock portfolio was selected with hindsight;
   S&P 100 membership is itself a winner-selection criterion. Both biases
   inflate forward returns. The S&P 100 test mitigates but does not
   eliminate survivorship bias. Fully unbiased would require Russell 3000
   with point-in-time membership — out of scope for one investor's tool.

3. **Lookback window:** 3 years (mid-2023 to mid-2026), predominantly
   bullish macro. Rule's performance under drawdown environments
   (e.g., 2022) is untested in this run.

4. **Multiple comparisons:** 4 rules × 3 horizons = 12 statistical tests.
   Bonferroni-corrected significance threshold is p<0.0042 (raw p<0.05 ÷ 12),
   equivalent to t≥2.69. Reported t-stats and p-values are uncorrected; see
   threshold heuristic for the corrected bar.

5. **p-value approximation:** Computed via normal approximation. Valid for
   large samples (which we have); slightly anti-conservative for small
   samples.

6. **Path dependency:** Forward-return windows overlap (e.g., +20d returns
   computed from consecutive days share 19 days of price action). Standard
   errors not adjusted for autocorrelation. Effect on directional verdict
   is minimal; absolute p-values may be slightly optimistic.

7. **Test B dip-fill detection (after fix):** Uses minimum close in 20-day
   window. Production catches dips that happen intraday but recover by
   close; this script does not. Test B fill rates may be biased downward
   relative to production reality.

## Verdict heuristic (applied to this run)

For a rule to be defensibly recommended for adoption:

- ≥100 MOMENTUM observations ✓ (all rules pass)
- Forward 20d return spread ≥ +2pp on the broader universe — **fails** for R0/R1/R2; **R3 only marginally passes** (+1.03pp)
- t-stat ≥ 2 in predicted direction on broader universe — **fails** for R0/R1/R2; R3 passes raw (t=2.18)
- t-stat ≥ 2.69 (Bonferroni) on broader universe — **fails** for all rules at +20d
- Sign consistent across horizons — **fails** for R0/R1/R2 (positive short-term, negative long-term)

**No rule clears all bars on the broader universe.** Status quo wins by
default — do not change the production rule based on this evidence.
